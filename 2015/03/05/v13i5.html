Thursday, 5 March 2015

International Edition

Volume 13, Issue 5

Editorial

Having won the Austrian Team Championship last week, TFC Royal 78 Kaisermühlen have gone on to lift the International Open of Prague's team title this week, although making it a treble by winning the FISTF Major in Mons this weekend may be a rather more difficult challenge!.

Headlines

Kegenbein thrashes Haas in Prague

German player Björn Kegenbein thrashed Austrian Christian Haas 4-1 to lift the first international tournament to be held in the Czech Republic in over a decade, with video footage of the entire final available online. An all-Austrian team final saw Royal 78 Kaisermühlen scrape a 1-0 victory over Wiener Neustadt.

SC Charleroi top Belgian Club Championships [fr]

Former European Club Champions SC Charleroi top the Belgian team league the week before the Major in Mons. They have the narrowest of leads over Rochefort TS, with AS Hennuyer the ‘surprise’ package near the bottom of the table. Lions Eugies have the peculiar honour of having their “A” team bottom of the 1st Division, whilst their “B” side tops Division 2!

Steve Bennett is Scottish Champion

Scotland becomes the first nation to crown a national champion this season, with Dundee’s Steve Bennett waltzing to victory at the Glasgow Open, having gained an unassailable lead in the Scottish circuit.
