Thursday, 15 October 2015

International Edition

Volume 14, Issue 6

Editorial

Apologies for the 'missing' Argus last week, I was unexpected left without a suitable internet connection. The fortnight has seen a lot of action, with Saverio Bari taking the Coppa Italia title after defeating Daniele Bertelli in the final, whilst two International Opens and the South American Championships have also taken place.

Headlines

Brazil dominate South American Championship

Brazil defeated Argentina 4-0 in the final of the South American Championships to draw the two nations level in the total number of victories in that competition. Meanwhile, CR Vasco da Gama topped a group including SE Palmeiras and AS Rosario to win the team competition, whilst reigning champion Ednilson Gaffo retained his individual crown, beating Fleix Londrina teammate Rony Suzuky 2-0 in the final.

Alberto Mateos lifts IO Estepa title

Alberto Mateos took the scalp of FISTF World Player of the Year Juan Noguera at the International Open of Estepa, defeating him 4-2 in the final ahead of a Spanish record field of 80 players and 16 teams. Meanwhile, the Spanish veteran side continued their winning streak, defeating a team from Gibraltar in an international friendly, whilst Tiborones FM took home the team title, cruising to a 3-0 victory over Belgian side Stembert.

Chris Thomas wins in Melbourne IO

Chris Thomas took the International Open of Melbourne trophy back to England with him, defeating his brother Peter, now based in Australia, 4-0. Both players had progressed comfortably to the final, with Peter defeating Elliot Kennedy 3-0 in one semi final, and Chris thrashing Fabrizio Coco 5-0 in the other.
