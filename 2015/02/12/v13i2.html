Editorial

The second issue of the Argus sees a good fortnight for both Christophe Dheur, who added the French GP title to his recent win at the Open d'Eugies, and Scotsman Steve Bennett who claimed victory at both the English IO in Enderby and in a regional tournament in Scotland. French club FTC Issy also continued to prosper, with the club putting on a huge GP the weekend after their players take the top 5 positions in the French ranking.

Headlines

SC Charleroi dominate French GP in Issy

With 162 players and 30 clubs in attendance, FTC Issy-les-Moulineaux have been making a strong claim for their historic tournament to be considered a FISTF Major. Belgian player Christophe Dheur claimed victories against world champion Juan Noguera in the semi-final and then fellow countryman Sami Targui in the final to lift the trophy. SC Charleroi put in a dominant performance in the team competition, beating Italian side Fiamme Azzurre in the final, with the B team making the semi-final.

London & Essex United win English IO

United scored a dramatic win against fellow English team Yorkshire Phoenix in the team final of the Enderby IO in England, with Scotland’s Steve Bennett lifting the open category title, defeating Portuguese expat Paolo Gouveia 3-1 in the final. Elsewhere, Belgian player Daniel Scheen narrowly beating Spaniard Luis Mendez in the veteran category, whilst Kevin Cordell and Richard Stock won in the plate competition.
