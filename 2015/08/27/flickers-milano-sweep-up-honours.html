International Edition

Volume 13, Issue 30

Editorial

Welcome to the final issue of volume 13! Whilst there's been a slowdown the European game, the Southern hemisphere has seen a surge in activity, with four tournaments taking place within a week in Australia. It's been a particularly good week for the Thomas family, with Chris winning the English Masters whilst his younger brother won the AGFA Cup on the other side of the world, in Sydney. Meanwhile, Flickers Milano players have been globetrotting in search of trophies, picking up titles in Japan and Finland.

Headlines

Yokohama GP title for Astengo

Details are somewhat sparse, but Flickers Milano’s Giulio Astengo won the International Grand Prix of Japan in Yokohama last weekend, taking the Open trophy back to Italy, as well as reaching the final of the veteran category. English translation

Depascale crowned champion at Helsinki IO

Flickers Milano made it a double, with Vittorio Depascale defeating Australian ex-pat Bob Green in the final, followed by a win against Giorgos Papadopoulos the following day to bag the veteran title too. English translation

Australian Subbuteo

There’s been an abundance of news from down under this week, with five of the country’s six clubs hosting tournaments. Having renounced their full membership of FISTF in 2001, it had appeared that the Antipodean game was on it’s last legs, but a resurgence in recent years has seen the association go from strength to strength.
