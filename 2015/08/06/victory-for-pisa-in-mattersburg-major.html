Editorial

This week has seen a tale of two cities played out in Italy. A triumphant Black & Blue Pisa continuing an incredible season by lifting three titles at the Mattersburg Major. Meanwhile, Saverio Bari has been rapidly assembling an Italian superclub in the form of Reggio Emilia, adding legendry player Renzo Frignani, fellow former World Champion Giancarlo Giulianini, and current golden boy Luca Zambello - who lifted both the Coppa Italia and Italian Championship titles earlier this year - to last week's signing of Massimilano Nastasi.

Victory for Pisa in Mattersburg Major

Black & Blue Pisa have continued their stellar season by lifting the team trophy, beating Fiamme Azzurre Roma 8-5 on goals scored in the final. The individual competition yielded another success for the Tuscans, with Daniele Bertelli defeating Catania IO winner Luca Colangelo 2-1 in the final, having defeated world champion Juan Noguera by the same score at the semi-final stage. Talking To Walker

This weekend, Zach Walker will attempt to equal Gregg Deinhart’s record of seven US National Championship titles, won between 1996 and 2004. Despite his impressive record, however, Walker feels that he’ll have his work cut out. He names US World Cup squad members Paul O’Donovan Rossa and Peter Alegi, former U19 champions Michael Tillman (2012) and Stuart Russell (2011), and home players Ilias Gikas and Erik Haefke amongst the strongest of the competition. And that’s before you get on to former national champions Daniel Cranston (2009, 2011) and Paul Eyes (2012)!

For a player who has seen so many top tournaments, you have to wonder which stick out as being the most memorable. “Certainly have been several”, comes the reply from the man from Colorado. It’s telling that, amongst the list of European Majors and FISTF World Cups come three tournaments closer to home; the 1999 and 2006 ASA National Championships, and the 2007 ASA Holiday Silver - the largest tournament ever held on American soil, attracting 66 players.

So what has he learnt in his 16 years at the top of the game? “Smile more while playing. I think you have to be serious and intense in this game in order to succeed, but having fun is the most important part”. Surely good advice for every player.
