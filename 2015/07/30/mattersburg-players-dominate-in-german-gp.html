Editorial

The past fortnight's been rather busy, with both the German and English GPs taking place. There's also been some major transfers, with Eagles Napoli losing Massimiliano Nastasi to Reggio Emilia, replacing him with SC Charleroi's Antoino Mettivieri.

Headlines

Leitner takes honours in Bavaria

TFC Mattersburg’s Wolfgang Leitner won the German GP in Rain last weekend, defeating fellow Austrian Alexander Haas. The club also lifted the team trophy, comfortably beating Belgian side SC Stembert in the final, having dispatched fellow Austrians Wiener Neustadt at the semi-final stage. In the categories, World number 1 Gianfranco Calonico (Italy) won the veteran category, with Austrian youngsters Lukas Eppensteiner (Under 15) and Daniel Vranovitz (Under 12) won the junior categories, and home player Arnold Mair came out on top in the plate competition. Excellent English GP for AS Hennuyer

75 players attended what must be seen as a warm-up for the FISTF Major to be held in England in 2016, with Belgian Under 19 star David Vantassel beating French AS Hennuyer teammate Remi Soret 2-1 after extra time in the final. With the veteran Belgian club providing both Open finalists, there was little shock when they also lifted the team competition, beating London & Essex United 3-1 in the final.

ASA National Championships

This year sees the 25th anniversary of the American association’s national championships, to be hosted in Chicago on 8th/9th August. The first championships were first played in 1982, becoming an annual event ten years later. Some readers with longer memories might remember the last official “Subbuteo” World Cup - run by Waddingtons in 1994 - was held in the city, Belgian player Dominique De Marco defeating France’s Christophe Fuseau 4-3 in the final.

The tournament is to be hosted by Chicago United TSC, which has grown to become one of the largest clubs in the United States since the merger of Midwest United SC and the Chicago Area SC last December. Zach Walker will face stiff competition as he looks to equal Gregg Deinhart’s record 7 titles at the event. A short interview with the former English Premier League player will follow next week.
