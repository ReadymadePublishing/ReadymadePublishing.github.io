Editorial

The 2014/15 season has finally drawn to a close, with Giorgos Zangylos (Cyprus) the last national champion to be crowned, along with Mark Farrell's victory in the all-Ireland championships. The season has seen several prominent players/organisers speak out against the lack of new blood being brought in to the game, a theme likely to continue for some time, whilst criticism of the World Cup being held annually has once again reared it's head.

Fayçal wins all-Rouis final at French IO

The French capital saw Fayçal Rouis defeat his brother, Mongi, 1-0 after extra time. It was by no means an easy passage for the Frenchman. Having scraped past Xavier Dubois in 1-0 the quarter finals, he also needed extra time to overcome Axel Donval 2-1 in the semis. Mark Offmann and Raphael Pommier won in the categories. Tartan Army on Tour Again

The forthcoming World Cup in San Benedetto will be the first World Cup held outside the UK since 2001 that has seen a Scottish team present. The team captain will be Mike Burns, who spent the last season at Italian club ACS Perugia following a dispute with the Scottish association. So why this year?

“Having played in every World Cup since 1998 I suppose it had lost a bit of its magic”, says Mike, “it was just another tournament on the calendar to go to”. Taking a line similar to the one Vasco Guimaraes had stated the month before, some of the blame for this was placed on the event being too regular, although he did add that there were a number of reasons why Scottish players haven’t ranked the event highly in recent years.

However, with changing circumstances for some of the Scottish squad, along with the opportunity for a holiday in the Italian sun, they have decided to attend. No doubt the choice has also been partially influenced by the possibility of five Hot Club d’Ecosse players being able to play together as a team again, the famous Edinburgh club having withdrawn from competition in their home nation.

But the big question is whether or not they’ll be wearing their iconic national dress: “As for our attire, we are getting some lovely new kits and it’s quite possible we’ll be voted the best dressed team”, beams Burnsie, “Kilts are optional - I have my own and might just take it …”
