Thursday, 10 December 2015

International Edition

Volume 14, Issue 12

Editorial

Apologies for the two week absence, I've been a little ill. Unfortunately, it has come in the middle of some sad news, that table football legend Vasco Guimaraes is to leave the game after a sparkling career that has seen him win everything from the televised 1990 Subbuteo World Cup, the FISTF World Championship and European Championship, Europa Cup, and numerous other titles.

Headlines

Mettivieri crowned champion at Berlin Grand Prix

Antonio Mettivieri defeated Italian player Saverio Bari 2-1 during extra time to lift the Berlin Grand Prix title, having overcome fellow Belgian Christopher Dhuer in the semi-finals. Meanwhile, Austrian player Stefan Sandner won the veterans title, Victoria Büsing the female category, and Alwin Krause won the U15 category, and was narrowly defeated in the U19 final by Fabio Bianco. Belgian club AS Hennuyer drew the team event final against a three man TFC Wiener Neustadt side, winning thanks to a bye for Olivier Pere.

All Greek for Hantzaras

Spyros Hantzaras defeated Lazaros Papakonstantinou to lift the Greek Grand Prix title, with surprise package Georgios Genitsaropoulous putting in an impressive performance to reach the semi-finals. Meanwhile, Falcons Athens overcame fierce rivals Atlas in the final of the team event.

Pawsey wins Glasgow Open

Fresh from topping the English National League the previous weekend, in-form Dave Pawsey won the International Open of Glasgow, defeating teammate Elliot Bellefontaine 4-1 in the final. Scottish legend David Baxter took home the veterans title after defeating Steve Bennett, whilst Yorkshire Phoenix pipped Dundee United to take the team title.

Ho stops Kenzo Koi’s dream in Yokohama

The game in East Asia continues to gain momentum, with 27 players competing for the Yokohama Open title. American player Andrea Gasparini came unstuck at the semi final stage against tournament organizer Kenzo Koi, with Singapore’s John Ho narrowly defeating Koi 1-0 in the final. Elsewhere, there were wins for Karibu Miwa (U15) and Kazunari Shigenobu (U19).
